Anatomy

The four optical zones

A good Liquid Glass approximation should not distort everything evenly. The center stays calm; the edge does the optical work.

1. Center frost

The center mostly uses blur, tint, saturation and contrast. It should preserve legibility and avoid obvious warping.

2. Inner shoulder

A mild magnification zone near the rim suggests depth without making the whole control swim.

3. Edge refraction

The strongest displacement lives near the outer 8–24px. This is where the background appears pinched or bent.

4. Specular highlight

A directional edge highlight and inner lowlight make the object feel like a physical lens rather than a drawn outline.

Implementation

Three levels of web implementation

Choose based on fidelity, browser support, performance budget and how much arbitrary CRM content sits behind the surface.

Level 1: CSS-only

Use blur, saturation, translucent fill, inner highlights and shadows. Fast and robust, but no true spatial distortion.

Level 2: fake rim zoom

Duplicate a controlled background inside the rim and scale it. Great for app backgrounds, less accurate over arbitrary DOM content.

Level 3: displacement

Use SVG filters or a shader with a displacement map. The center is neutral; the rim shifts pixels along the edge normal.

Use lensing on

  • Global navigation and command search
  • Floating CRM action toolbars
  • Quick-create menus and popovers
  • Selected action groups and primary actions

Avoid lensing on

  • Data tables and table rows
  • Kanban/deal cards in steady state
  • Long forms, emails, notes and timelines
  • Charts and core dashboard content
Checklist

What to check before shipping

The effect should feel optical and useful, not decorative or distracting.

Rim only. Keep distortion strongest near the outer 8–24px; the center should remain calm.
Dense data stays stable. Tables, forms and charts should not warp underneath every row.
Use adaptive strength. Big panels can appear thicker; tiny controls should be lighter.
Do not stack glass on glass. One material container can hold ordinary controls.
Test real CRM backgrounds. Try it over tables, charts, selected rows, empty states and error banners.
Provide fallbacks. Reduced transparency and high contrast should become solid, readable surfaces.
